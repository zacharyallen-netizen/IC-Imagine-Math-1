1 Midpoint & Endpoint

📌 Midpoint = average of the coordinates: ( (x₁+x₂)/2 , (y₁+y₂)/2 ). To find a missing endpoint, work backward.
Midpoint of A(−2,1) and B(4,5) is M(1,3)

Midpoint of (2, 3) and (8, 7)

Average each: ((2+8)/2 , (3+7)/2) = click to reveal (5, 5)

2 Distance Formula

📌 d = √( (x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)² ) — the Pythagorean Theorem on the plane: legs Δx and Δy, hypotenuse d.
From (1,1) to (4,5): legs 3 and 4 → d = 5

Distance between (1, 2) and (4, 6)

Δx = 3, Δy = 4: √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = click to reveal √25

4 Parallel & Perpendicular Slopes

📌 Parallel → SAME slope. Perpendicular → OPPOSITE RECIPROCAL slopes (their product is −1).
Two parallel lines (slope 2) and a perpendicular one (slope −½)

5 Equations of Parallel/Perp Lines

📌 Keep the slope (parallel) or take the opposite reciprocal (perpendicular), then write the equation through the given point.

Line through (0, 1) parallel to y = 2x − 5

Parallel → same slope m = 2; through (0,1) → b = 1: click to reveal y = 2x + 1

Line through (0, 1) perpendicular to y = 2x − 5

Perpendicular → slope = −½; through (0,1): click to reveal y = −½x + 1
