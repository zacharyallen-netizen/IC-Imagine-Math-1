3 Outliers

📌 A value is an outlier if it is below Q1 − 1.5·IQR or above Q3 + 1.5·IQR. Outliers pull the mean, not the median.
Because the median resists outliers, it’s often the fairer ‘typical’ value for skewed data (like income).

4 Scatter Plots & Regression

📌 The line of best fit ŷ = a + bx models the trend. Slope = predicted change per unit; intercept = predicted value at x = 0. Interpolate inside the data; extrapolate beyond it (risky).

5 Residuals & Correlation

📌 Residual = actual − predicted. A good fit has small residuals scattered randomly. Correlation r is from −1 to 1: near ±1 = strong, near 0 = weak; the sign gives the direction.
Residuals (dashed) = vertical gaps from the line

✓ Residual = actual − predicted = 14 − 12 = click to reveal +2

6 Correlation vs. Causation

⚠️ Correlation is NOT causation. A strong relationship doesn’t prove one variable causes the other — a hidden lurking variable may explain both.
Ice-cream sales and shark attacks rise together — but hot weather drives both. Neither causes the other.
