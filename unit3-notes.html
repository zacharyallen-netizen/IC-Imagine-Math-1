1 Solutions & the Graph

2 Solving by Graphing

📌 The solution to a system is the intersection point — where the lines cross.
One solution: lines cross
No solution: parallel
Infinitely many: same line

Solve y = 2x − 1 and y = −x + 5 by graphing/setting equal

Set equal: 2x − 1 = −x + 5 → click to reveal 3x = 6
Solve x, then y: x = 2 → click to reveal y = 3
✓ Solution: click to reveal (2, 3)

Two gyms: A costs 2x + 4, B costs x + 7 (x = visits). When is the cost the same?

Set equal & solve: 2x + 4 = x + 7 → click to reveal x = 3
✓ Cost at x = 3: 2(3) + 4 = click to reveal $10 — they meet at (3, 10)
The lines cross at (3, 10): 3 visits cost $10 at either gym

3 Substitution

📌 When one variable is alone, substitute its expression into the other equation.

Solve y = 2x and x + y = 9

Substitute y = 2x: x + 2x = 9 → click to reveal 3x = 9
Solve x: click to reveal x = 3
✓ Back-substitute: y = 2(3) → click to reveal y = 6 → (3, 6)

4 Elimination & Why It Works

📌 Adding equal quantities to both sides keeps equality — that’s why elimination produces the same solution. Line up the system so one variable cancels.

Solve x + y = 10 and x − y = 4

Add the equations (y cancels): click to reveal 2x = 14
Solve x: click to reveal x = 7
✓ Substitute back: 7 + y = 10 → click to reveal y = 3 → (7, 3)

5 Choosing a Method

📌 Pick the most efficient tool for the system in front of you.
How to choose: substitution, elimination, or graphing

6 Graphing Inequalities & Systems

📌 Boundary line: solid for ≤ or ≥, dashed for < or >. Shade the side that makes it true; for a system, the solution is the overlap.
x + y ≤ 6 : solid line, shade below; (1,1) is a solution
📌 SYSTEM of inequalities: graph both lines, then the answer is the overlap — where the two shadings cover the same spot.
y ≥ x − 1 AND y ≤ −x + 3 — the solution is the shaded overlap
